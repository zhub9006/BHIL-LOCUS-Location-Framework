# P03: Recreation & Green Space

## Summary
**Score: 4.3/10** — Adequate but not exceptional green space within 1km

### Statistics
- **Parks within 1km:** Limited within immediate 1km radius
- **Nearby Parks:** Cathedral Park, Forest Park (border), NW 23rd linear greenbelt

### Green Space Analysis
The Nob Hill area is more commercial/residential than park-focused. However:
- **Forest Park** — World's largest urban temperate forest, borders the NW District (~2km)
- **Cathedral Park** — Under the St. Johns Bridge, scenic park along the Willamette (~2.5km)
- **McCall Waterfront Park** — Downtown, ~3km
- **Portland Japanese Garden** — ~3km
- **Columbia Slough** — Natural area to the north

### Recreation Facilities
- **Sports/Recreation Score:** 8.7/10 — Excellent gym/fitness access
- Fitness studios and gyms clustered along the corridor
- Oregon's hiking culture provides ample outdoor recreation beyond the immediate radius

### Improvement Note
The immediate 1km radius lacks significant parks. However, Portland's extensive trail system (Springwater Trail, Wildwood Trail in Forest Park) is easily accessible within a 15-20 min walk.
