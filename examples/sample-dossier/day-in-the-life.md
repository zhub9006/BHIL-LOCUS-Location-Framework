## A Typical Weekday

**6:45 AM** — Morning starts with the ambient hum of Capitol Hill waking up.
The Pike/Pine corridor is already stirring. A short walk (0.1 mi) to Victrola
Coffee on Pine gets the day started. The resident doesn't own a car — they don't
need one.

**7:30 AM** — Commute to downtown. Options this morning: walk to Capitol Hill
Link Light Rail Station (0.3 mi, ~6 min), ride one stop to Westlake, arrive
at a downtown office in under 15 minutes total. Alternatively, a 12-minute
bike ride via the Broadway protected lane.

**12:00 PM** — Lunch options are essentially unlimited. Thai, Japanese, Mexican,
Mediterranean, a dozen more — all within the Pike/Pine corridor. Today it's
Stateside on E Pine (modern Vietnamese, 0.1 mi). In and out in 40 minutes.

**5:30 PM** — Work ends. Quick grocery stop at QFC on Broadway (0.2 mi) on the
walk home. Full-service grocery, no car trip required.

**6:30 PM** — Cal Anderson Park for an evening run. The park's reservoir loop
and open lawn are 0.1 mi from the address. There's always activity — dog walkers,
pickup basketball, picnickers. Seattle's long summer evenings make this a
near-daily option May through September.

**8:00 PM** — Dinner or drinks with friends. The Pike/Pine corridor offers
essentially any vibe: quiet craft cocktails at Barboza, loud EDM at Neighbours,
wine at a dozen small bars, food late into the night. A cab or rideshare home
is never more than 4 minutes away.

**10:30 PM** — Home. The address is on Pike, so ambient noise from the corridor
is real. Light sleepers should note this; the density that makes D2 score 9.4
is the same density generating the 10pm soundscape.

## A Typical Weekend

**Saturday:** Farmer's market at Capitol Hill Farmers Market (seasonal, 0.4 mi).
Coffee and a long walk through the neighborhood. If heading to a different
part of the city — Ballard, Fremont, West Seattle — the bus network handles it.
For Green Lake or hiking access, car rental or Zipcar (0.2 mi away) covers the gap.

**Sunday:** The neighborhood is calmer. Brunch crowds at many spots by 10am.
A good day for a longer ride on the Burke-Gilman or a trip to Volunteer Park
(0.6 mi) for the Conservatory and city views.
