## Dimension Narratives

### D1 — Walkability & Geospatial (9.7 / 10)

Capitol Hill's Pike/Pine ridge is the highest-scoring walkability zone in Seattle.
Walk Score 97, with virtually every daily need within 0.25 miles. The street grid
is dense, sidewalks are continuous, and the corridor has pedestrian overlay zoning
enforcing active street-level uses. Terrain is moderate (hill crest); flat in most
directions. FEMA Zone X — minimal flood exposure.

### D2 — Food, Drink & Social (9.4 / 10)

40+ restaurants and 25+ bars/cafes within 0.25 miles. Cuisine diversity is
exceptional: Vietnamese, Japanese, Thai, Mexican, Mediterranean, American, and
more. Nightlife options span every archetype — craft cocktails, dive bars, dance
clubs, live music. This is the highest-density food and social corridor in Seattle
outside of Pike Place Market (which is commercial, not residential). Weekend brunch
wait times are real at popular spots.

### D3 — Recreation & Green Space (7.8 / 10)

Cal Anderson Park (2.4 acres, 0.1 mi) is a high-quality urban green space with
reservoir feature, open lawn, tennis/basketball courts, and frequent community
events. Volunteer Park (10 acres, 0.6 mi) offers city views and the Conservatory.
The deficit is the absence of large regional park access within walking distance —
Seward Park, Lincoln Park, and mountain trailheads require transit or a vehicle.
For a Young Professional persona, this scores well because D3 weight is 1.0×.

### D4 — Daily Errands & Retail (9.1 / 10)

Full-service QFC grocery (0.2 mi), Bartell Drugs pharmacy (0.1 mi), hardware
(0.4 mi), and specialty food options in every direction. The corridor supports
truly car-free daily life. Pharmacy, dry cleaning, banking, postal services — all
walkable. The only gap is large-format retail (Costco-style), which requires a
vehicle or transit.

### D5 — Education Profile (6.2 / 10)

Public elementary school assignment (Stevens, 0.6 mi) is rated above average
but not top-tier by GreatSchools metrics. Middle and high school access is
adequate. Seattle Central College (0.4 mi) is a plus for community college
access. For a Young Professional without children, this dimension is discounted
to 0.5× weight — it has minimal impact on composite scoring and daily life
quality.

### D6 — Transportation & Commute (9.3 / 10)

Capitol Hill Link Light Rail Station (Sound Transit 1 Line) is 0.3 mi away —
a 6-minute walk. One stop to Westlake/downtown; direct service to SeaTac Airport.
King County Metro provides 5+ bus routes within 2 blocks. Bike infrastructure
includes the Broadway protected lane (N-S) and Pine St bike route (E-W). Commute
to downtown: 12–18 minutes transit, 15 minutes bike. Car ownership unnecessary
for most lifestyles. This is among the top 4% of all Seattle addresses for
transit connectivity.

### D7 — Civic & Infrastructure (7.1 / 10)

Seattle City Light and Seattle Public Utilities serve the area with above-average
reliability. Infrastructure age is moderate — Capitol Hill has mid-century and
older utility networks under active investment programs. Police response times
are within city median. The neighborhood has elevated property crime metrics
consistent with a dense commercial corridor (vehicle break-ins, retail theft);
violent crime rates are not elevated versus MSA baseline. Fire station coverage
is within 1.0 mi.

### D8 — Demographics & Trajectory (7.9 / 10)

Capitol Hill is a mature gentrified neighborhood — it passed peak transition in
the 2010s. The current trajectory is stable: new construction is occurring but
not at displacement-scale rates. Median household income is above city median.
Population is younger (median age ~32) and highly educated. Commercial vacancy
is low. The neighborhood has established community identity and stable social
infrastructure. Upward price pressure continues but at a moderate pace.

### D9 — Business Environment (8.6 / 10)

Capitol Hill's commercial corridor is among the healthiest in Seattle. Low
vacancy, diversified tenant mix, strong foot traffic. The neighborhood supports
independent businesses well — this is not a chain-dominated corridor. CoStar
data indicates positive net absorption. The area's vibrancy is a leading
indicator of continued residential demand. The business environment directly
supports D2 (food/social) scores by sustaining the density of options.
